## The arc

I started where most engineers start — HTML, CSS, JavaScript, building things people could *see*. Then I got curious about the layer underneath: not just what a product looks like, but what it knows, how it learns, and what happens when the data gets messy.

That curiosity pulled me through UI/UX internships, into ETL pipelines and SQL dashboards, and eventually into deep learning, graph neural networks, and RAG systems. Today I sit somewhere most people don't: I understand both the frontend of a product and the ML pipeline feeding it — and I think that's increasingly where the interesting problems live.

I'm now actively moving toward **ML/AI Engineering** — building models that don't just train well but deploy cleanly, scale gracefully, and fail in ways you can actually debug.

## Projects that mark the journey

### 🌿 LeafHydroNet — Plant Health Detection System
*My strongest project. The one I'd walk into any interview with.*

Real-time leaf hydration and health detection using a CNN backbone paired with YOLOv8n for object detection, wrapped in a Streamlit dashboard for live inference. The challenge wasn't the model — it was making the pipeline fast enough to be usable in the field, and interpretable enough to be trusted by someone who isn't a data scientist.

`CNN` `YOLOv8n` `Streamlit` `Python` `Computer Vision`

---

### ⚡ SHPIPE — Self-Healing Data Pipeline *(w/ Merril Baiju, Abdur Rahuman)*
*Because pipelines that silently break at 3am are everyone's problem.*

**99.5% ML accuracy · GAT loss → 0.0024 · 80% MTTR reduction**

Uses Graph Attention Networks to model pipeline topology and detect failure signatures, with PPO reinforcement learning to autonomously reroute and recover — before a human even gets alerted. This project taught me what "production-grade" actually means versus what we pretend it means in coursework.

`GNNs` `GAT` `PPO` `NetworkX` `Python`

---

### 🔬 ResearchRAG — Literature Gap Intelligence
*Submitted to the Unstop Innovation Challenge · Round 1*

Researchers spend weeks reading to find what hasn't been said yet. ResearchRAG takes a collection of uploaded academic papers and surfaces literature gaps, generates related work drafts, and highlights under-explored angles — using RAG architecture over a custom document index.

Built to solve a problem I experienced firsthand while doing academic work. Still in progress, but the Round 1 submission is in.

`RAG` `LLMs` `Vector Databases` `NLP` `Python`

---

### 🧬 Codon-Helix — Quantum-Inspired DNA Compression
*Patent disclosure filed · Bioinformatics × compression research*

The most unconventional thing in my portfolio — and deliberately so. Codon-Helix is a quantum-inspired framework for compressing DNA sequences, combining domain knowledge from bioinformatics with algorithmic compression techniques. Completed through the full patent disclosure process including prior art search and IDF-B filing.

It's here because it represents something important: I'm not afraid of problems that don't have a tutorial.

`Bioinformatics` `Compression Algorithms` `Research` `Python`
